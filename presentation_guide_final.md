# 15-Minute Presentation Guide: LP as New Derivative Instrument

## Framing: Capability Building, Not Revenue Pitch

**Core Message**: "We now have a new type of derivative to work with. Here's what it is and why it matters."

**Tone**: Matter-of-fact, technical, educational
- NOT: "Exciting opportunity to make money!"
- YES: "Crypto derivatives are maturing. We need to understand them."

**Context**: Clients need on-chain solutions, so we need this capability
- NOT: "We can win $1M in new revenue!"
- YES: "Some clients want on-chain execution. We need to serve that need professionally."

---

## Time Allocation (15 Minutes)

- **Opening + Context**: 2 min - "New derivative in our toolbox"
- **What It Is**: 3 min - LP mechanics and the discovery
- **How It Works**: 5 min - Greeks, risk management, when to use
- **What We're Doing**: 3 min - The $1M initiative and infrastructure
- **Takeaways**: 1 min - Crypto maturity narrative
- **Q&A**: 1 min

## Anticipated Questions & Answers

### Q: "Is this really necessary? Can't clients just use centralized exchanges?"

**A (45 sec):** "Some can, and should. But some clients have regulatory or custody requirements that make on-chain execution preferable or mandatory. We're seeing this with protocol treasuries, certain institutional mandates, and regulatory frameworks in some jurisdictions. We need to be able to serve these needs professionally, not tell them 'we don't do that.' It's about having the full toolkit."

---

### Q: "How big will this be as a percentage of our business?"

**A (30 sec):** "Not projecting this as a major revenue driver immediately. It's more about strategic capability. Right now it's $1M for infrastructure and learning. Could scale over time as client needs grow and crypto markets mature, but that's not the primary rationale. Primary rationale is having the capability when clients need it."

---

### Q: "What if DeFi gets regulated out of existence?"

**A (45 sec):** "Possible, but unlikely given current trajectory. Major jurisdictions are creating frameworks to regulate, not ban. EU's MiCA, for example, provides legal clarity. If regulatory landscape shifts dramatically, we can wind down in days‚Äîpositions are liquid. But the trend is toward legitimization and regulation, not prohibition. Either way, better to understand these instruments and not need them than need them and not understand them."

---

### Q: "This seems different from our core business."

**A (45 sec):** "Respectfully disagree. Our core business is derivatives trading and risk management. These are derivatives‚Äîthey just trade on-chain instead of on CME or Deribit. The math is the same. The risk framework is the same. The only difference is execution venue. It's like when we started trading crypto futures on CME‚Äîsame derivative concepts, new venue. This is an evolution of capabilities, not a pivot."

---

### Q: "What's the smart contract risk?"

**A (30 sec):** "Real risk, but Uniswap v3 has $4B TVL and has been live for 3+ years with no exploits. Extensively audited. We're also using position limits‚Äîmax 20% of any pool, diversification across pools. Similar to how we manage counterparty risk with exchanges. It's manageable, not eliminable."

---

### Q: "Why not wait until this is more mature?"

**A (45 sec):** "Two reasons: One, clients are asking for this now‚Äîwe can't tell them to wait. Two, expertise building takes time. By the time this is 'mature' in 2-3 years, firms that built capability now will be established providers. Those starting then will be playing catch-up. We saw this with crypto futures‚Äîearly movers had advantages. Not predicting massive growth, but being ready."

---

### Q: "How does this fit with our existing derivatives business?"

**A (30 sec):** "Integrates well. We can use CEX futures to hedge DEX LP positions. Creates cross-venue arbitrage opportunities. Allows us to manage client assets holistically‚Äîsome on CEX, some on DEX as needed. It's additive, not either/or. Fuller toolkit means better client service."
