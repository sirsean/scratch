### Q: "Why do clients need us? Can't they just deposit themselves?"

**A (45 sec):** "They can deposit, but they can't: 1) Dynamically hedge delta with perps (requires CEX accounts and 24/7 monitoring), 2) Optimize ranges based on volatility regimes (requires derivatives expertise), 3) Integrate custody and reporting (institutional requirements), 4) Navigate tax implications (complex for IL). ### Q: "What happens in a bear market when yields drop?"

**A (45 sec):** "Two scenarios: 1) Volume drops‚Üífees drop‚Üíwe adjust ranges or fee tiers, similar to adjusting options strategies in low-vol markets. 2) Strong trends‚Üíimpermanent loss‚Üíwe exit positions or widen ranges. The strategy is regime-dependent, which is why clients pay us‚Äîthey don't want to manage this themselves."

**A (30 sec):** "Complements perfectly. We're already market making on CEX. This adds DEX market making for clients who want on-chain execution. ## Delivery Tips for "Update" vs "Proposal"

### Do:
- **Be confident** - This IS happening
- **Use "we're doing" not "we want to do"**
- **Show momentum** - "Already deployed $1M, first clients Q2"
- **Focus on revenue** - This is a business opportunity
- **Emphasize client demand** - Pull, not push strategy

### Don't:
- **Don't** sound tentative or experimental
- **Don't** ask for permission (you're informing, not asking)
- **Don't** oversell (let numbers speak)
- **Don't** defend against imagined objections
- **Don't** apologize for anything

### Tone Calibration:

**NOT**: "We think maybe this could work if we try it..."
**YES**: "We're launching this because clients are asking for it and we're uniquely positioned to win."

**NOT**: "Can we get approval to explore...?"
**YES**: "Here's what we're building and why it's strategic."
